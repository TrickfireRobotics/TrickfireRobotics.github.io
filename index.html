TRICKFIRE

ROBOTICS

TrickFire Robotics is a student-led robotics team focused on providing a unique educational experience through hands-on work on large scale projects. Since 2016 the team has traveled to the Kennedy Space Center to represent the University of Washington Bothell at the annual NASA Robotics Mining Competition. A competition that pits 50 college teams to design and build a rover that can mine on the surface of the moon.

The Mechanical team focuses on the physical design and assembly of the robot. The team faces the challenge of designing a rover that can mine up to its height below the surface while keeping the weight to under 60kg. The team succeeds in producing an efficient rover by incorporating various light weight composites with elegant design.

The electrical team takes what the mechanical team and software team have made, and it bring it all together effectively. They focus on designing high level power system architectures and PCBs for power distribution and electrical circuit safety.

The software team works on the current rover and ensures that the rover can work on the correct algorithm for competition. Software also works on low level CAN controls and looking at ways of improving our current and past rovers.

Our other subteams, Outreach and finance work together to ensure our team has enough funding for competition and our rover, recruiting new members, keeping an online presence in the digital world, and outreaching to work with local organizations in the Washington area.
