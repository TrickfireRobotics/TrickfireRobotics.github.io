Overview of Rovers

The NASA Robotics Mining Competition is a competition hosted by NASA, where 50 teams across the United States compete to build the best rover that can mine under the surface of the moon. A Rover starts at the home base, navigates through obstacles, and then reaches a mining zone where it has to break through a few layers. It then digs out regolith (gravel) and transports it back to the home base, where it deposits the regolith. The process is repeated multiple times.

In six years, TrickFire has built four different rovers, nearly building a new one for each year’s competition. Our most recent rover is named “Houdini,” as an association to the “Trick” in “TrickFire.”

Houdini

Houdini, the third iteration of the excavator/dump trunk/tread design, . With the NASA officially launching their Artemis program, the NASA RMC has shifted its focus to Lunar mining. In response our newest iteration is a third of its predecessors size, while being able being able to mine 20% deeper due to our redesign of the arms kinematics.

Atlas

Atlas is the next iteration of the excavator/dump trunk/tread design. Although the fundamental design is the same the team completely overhaled the rover adding in a reinforced carbon fiber frame, sailcloth dumping bin, and redesigning the drivetrain suspension. Atlas, now with a light carbon fiber frame, weighs in at 67 kg, a significant reduction to prior iterations.

Grimlock

Our first mars rover capable of mining past the powdery topsoil getting to the icy regolith 30cm underneath. Grimlock boasted a fully dynamic digging arm with 4 degrees of freedom, similar to a classic excavator, with a dump truck style bin that was used to transport the mined regolith, and tank treads that can drive over any obstacle that could be thrown at it. With an all-aluminum chassis Grimlock weighed in at just shy of 80 kilograms.

Daybreak

Our entry into the world of extraterrestrial mining, Daybreak was a four wheeled drum digger capable of mining 15 cm below the surface of Mars. This was back when the mission included collecting material from the top layers. The rover featured an all-aluminum frame weighing in at 71 kilograms.
