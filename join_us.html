Why Join?

The NASA Robotics Mining Competition is a competition hosted by NASA, where 50 teams across the United States compete to build the best rover that can mine under the surface of the moon. A Rover starts at the home base, navigates through obstacles, and then reaches a mining zone where it has to break through a few layers. It then digs out regolith (gravel) and transports it back to the home base, where it deposits the regolith. The process is repeated multiple times.

In six years, TrickFire has built four different rovers, nearly building a new one for each year’s competition. Our most recent rover is named “Houdini,” as an association to the “Trick” in “TrickFire.”
